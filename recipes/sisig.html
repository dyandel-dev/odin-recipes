Filipino Sisig Recipe

Description

Filipino sisig is a sizzling, savory, and tangy dish made with chopped pork (usually pig's face or belly), seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili. It's often served on a hot plate—perfect with rice or as a pulutan (bar food).

Ingredients

  • 500g pork belly (or pork face, if available)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 calamansi or 1 lemon (juiced)
  • 1-2 chili peppers, chopped (optional for spice)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp mayonnaise (optional, for creamy style)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 egg (optional, for sizzling plate topping)

Steps

  1. Boil the pork belly until tender (about 30-40 mins). Then grill or pan-fry until crispy. Let it cool, then chop into small pieces.
  2. In a pan, heat oil. Sauté the chopped pork with onions, soy sauce, calamansi juice, and chili. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. For a creamy sisig, mix in a spoonful of mayonnaise and stir well.
  4. Transfer to a hot plate. Optionally, crack an egg on top and let it cook while serving. Enjoy with rice or as a side dish!
